    What is a Brain Tumor?

    A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells within the brain. Some tumors are benign (non-cancerous), while others can be malignant (cancerous). Early detection and treatment are crucial for better outcomes.

    Common Types of Brain Tumors:

    1. Gliomas

      Gliomas originate from glial cells, which support and protect neurons in the brain. They can be fast-growing and affect brain function, depending on their size and location. Gliomas are one of the most common types of brain tumors.

    2. Meningiomas

      Meningiomas form in the meninges, the protective membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord. These tumors are usually benign and slow-growing, but their size and location can cause neurological problems.

    3. Pituitary Tumors

      Pituitary tumors develop in the pituitary gland, which regulates vital hormones. Most pituitary tumors are benign but can disrupt hormonal balance, leading to various health issues.
